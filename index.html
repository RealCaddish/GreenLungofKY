Examining Park Space per Person: Where do people have the most park space available?

A map by Nathaniel Deaton in the UKY Digital Mapping Program

This project aims to solve a spatial analysis question: How much park acreage is available per person in each census tract in Kentucky? In order to answer this question, I first collected data from sources such as the U.S. Census Bureau's ASC datasets and OpenStreetMap for municipal park vectors. Conduction of the spatial analysis was done in PostgreSQL and PostGIS.

You may notice that there are large swaths of state and national park datasets that aren't being analyzed. I decided for the purposes of this project to stick with using just municipal parks layers to keep it short and sweet. Additionally, as a control, I think it would be wise to assess municipal parks instead of state and national parks included because we can attain a lighter, simpler understanding of how municipalities think about their local parks systems.

Tools used in this project

  • U.S. Census Bureau ACS Datasets.
  • PostGIS.
